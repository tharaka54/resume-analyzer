1.Project Overview 
Project Name: HireIQ — Intelligent CV Screening & Skill Assessment Platform 
 
Project Goal: To develop a secure, AI-powered web platform that enables any user to publicly 
browse job postings, post job vacancies, and automatically screen applicants through role-specific 
AI-generated quizzes and a hybrid NLP-based CV ranking engine combining TF-IDF and BERT 
semantic similarity, enabling job posters to identify the most qualified candidates efficiently and 
objectively across any industry.

2.Background 
The current recruitment world is facing a dire situation of efficiency crisis. Studies by TheLadders 
(2012) show that an average recruiter spends 6 seconds to look at a particular resume at an initial 
stage; however, a significant percentage of those applications are sifted before a hiring manager. 
This shallow, quick screening creates a paradox, where large numbers of unqualified applicants 
flood the human resources departments, whilst really talented applicants are usually lost due to 
inconsistent and subjective screening techniques by human resources. 
This problem is significant. The Society of Human Resource Management (SHRM) estimates it to 
be about 17,000 $ on average to make a poor hire, whereas the time it takes to complete a single 
vacancy is 42 days on average (SHRM, 2022). The hiring process may be significantly extended 
in technical sectors with high demand (software engineering, data science, and cybersecurity). 
According to the ISC2 Cybersecurity Workforce Study (2023), it is estimated that around the 
world, there exist 3.5 million cybersecurity specialists, so there is a strong need to implement faster 
and more precise screening systems to match talent with roles.

The traditional Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS) used by over 98 % of the Fortune 500 
companies (Jobscan, 2023) are based on inflexible keyword matching that does not measure 
competence of a job applicant. A study conducted at Harvard Business School revealed that 88% 
of employers also recognize that their ATS automatically excludes qualified applicants only due 
to the lack of precise keyword patterns in their CVs, a process known as the hidden worker problem 
(Fuller et al., 2021). This is therefore a systematic lockout of competent candidates by the 
employers based on tooling limitation instead of actual skills gap. 
In addition to the accuracy of screening there is the growing concern of assessment integrity. In a 
2023 survey released by the talent assessment company HireVue, 52% of recruiters said that they 
had encountered candidates who lied about their skills on their CVs, and 38 % of recruiters said 
that an applicant had been coached in advance on standard interview questions (HireVue, 2023). 
Without a goal, tamper-resistant skills checking measure early into the recruitment pipeline, 
organizations run the risk of propelling the wrongly qualified or dishonest applicant to an 
expensive and time-sensitive interview. 
These are the challenges that HireIQ is dealing with. The platform replaces the subjective manual 
screening process with a reproducible, data-driven pipeline, enforced server-side with anti-
cheating measures, and with an anti-cheating hybrid NLP-based CV ranking engine (TF-IDF + 
BERT semantic similarity) which can be applied to any job role and industry. The result is a more 
expedited, unbiased and precise first round evaluation procedure that costs less, lowers recruiter 
load, and reveals the most truly qualified applicants. 
3.Project Scope 
3.1 Proposed Work 
HireIQ is a full-stack web site that integrates the totality of the recruitment pre-screening 
experience, inclusive of public job browsing, authenticated job posting, AI-based quiz-based 
assessment, secure, curricula vitae (CV) uploading of qualified applicants, a hybrid natural 
language processing (NLP)-based CV ranking system, and a structured recruiter dashboard. The 
system is designed to accommodate any job position and sector with AI generated content being 
dynamically tailored to fit the description of the specific job. 
3.2 In-Scope Features 
1.  Public job listings homepage with search and filter by title, location, and job type, no login 
required to browse view. 
2. Authentication is performed using Google OAuth 2.0, cross-site request forgery (CSRF) state 
token verification is used to prevent the loss of the session, and the user is allowed to register 
without going through steps of manual registration. 
3. Authenticated users can post job vacancies with title, company, description, location, job 
type, and required skills. 
4. AI quiz generation (Google Gemini 2.5 Flash) of 15 questions per job posting, each applicant 
receives a randomized selection of 15 with shuffled answers.

5. The quiz has a time limit of five minutes on the server which is strictly observed; otherwise 
the submissions beyond the time-limit are rejected and scored zero. 
6. Tab-switch detection during quiz 3 switches trigger automatic submission. 
7. Server-side quiz scoring correct answers are never transmitted to the frontend. 
8. CV upload pipeline gated behind a passing quiz score (12/15 or above), with retry limits and 
cooldown timers. 
9. Each PDF CV upload is checked through a complete six-tier security validation pipeline, 
which makes sure that all the data uploads meet the data integrity and privacy criteria.  
10. TF-IDF keyword matches scoring (32% weight) and BERT semantic similarity scoring (48% 
weight) per CV against the job description. (Combined hybrid ranking formula: Quiz 20% + 
TF-IDF 32% + BERT 48%.) 
11. TF-IDF keyword matches scoring (32% weight) and BERT semantic similarity scoring (48% 
weight) per CV against the job description. (Combined hybrid ranking formula: Quiz 20% + 
TF-IDF 32% + BERT 48%.) 
12. Gemini 2.5 Flash generates a plain English explanatory narrative of every applicant, falling 
back to rule-based content availability. 
13. Real-time WebSocket ranking progress for live scoring updates as CVs are processed. 
14. Resume viewer with dual-layer keyword and semantic sentence highlighting. 
15. CSV export of full applicant rankings including quiz scores. 
3.3 Out-of-Scope Features 
1. The system will not integrate with physical hardware devices such as webcams, biometric 
scanners or any external IoT/HR hardware. 
2. The system will not comply with enterprise-level regulations such as full GDPR/CCPA 
audits or ISO 27001 certification processes. 
3. The system will not include monetization or commercial features such as job ad marketplaces 
or advertising integrations. 
4. The system will not implement deep behavioral fraud detection such as keystroke dynamics, 
mouse movement analytics or webcam proctoring. 
5. The system will not support multiple languages (English only in this version.) 
6. The system will not connect to external corporate systems such as HRIS, LDAP/Active 
Directory or payroll/ERP platforms.

7.Possible Future Improvements 
 Development of a native mobile application for Android and iOS to allow candidates 
to browse jobs, take quizzes and track application status on mobile devices, with push 
notifications for job posters when new CVs are submitted. 
 Expansion to support skills-based hiring through dynamic skill graphing, automated 
upskilling resource suggestions for candidates with identified gaps, and AI-generated 
personalized job recommendations based on a candidate's existing skill profile. 
 Development of a predictive hire success scoring model trained on historical hiring 
outcomes to forecast a candidate's long-term performance in a role beyond the initial 
CV and quiz assessment. 
 Addition of a practical skills assessment module supporting role-specific coding 
challenges, scenario-based tasks and short-answer questions with AI evaluation, 
allowing job posters to verify hands-on technical ability before progressing candidates 
to final interviews.
