2.Background 

The current recruitment world is facing a dire situation of efficiency crisis. Studies by TheLadders (2012) show that an average recruiter spends 6 seconds to look at a particular resume at an initial stage; however, a significant percentage of those applications are sifted before a hiring manager. This shallow, quick screening creates a paradox, where large numbers of unqualified applicants flood the human resources departments, whilst really talented applicants are usually lost due to inconsistent and subjective screening techniques by human resources. This problem is significant. The Society of Human Resource Management (SHRM) estimates it to be about 17,000 $ on average to make a poor hire, whereas the time it takes to complete a single vacancy is 42 days on average (SHRM, 2022). The hiring process may be significantly extended in technical sectors with high demand (software engineering, data science, and cybersecurity). According to the ISC2 Cybersecurity Workforce Study (2023), it is estimated that around the world, there exist 3.5 million cybersecurity specialists, so there is a strong need to implement faster and more precise screening systems to match talent with roles. 
The traditional Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS) used by over 98 % of the Fortune 500 companies (Jobscan, 2023) are based on inflexible keyword matching that does not measure competence of a job applicant. A study conducted at Harvard Business School revealed that 88% 
of employers also recognize that their ATS automatically excludes qualified applicants only due to the lack of precise keyword patterns in their CVs, a process known as the hidden worker problem (Fuller et al., 2021). This is therefore a systematic lockout of competent candidates by the 
employers based on tooling limitation instead of actual skills gap. In addition to the accuracy of screening there is the growing concern of assessment integrity. In a 2023 survey released by the talent assessment company HireVue, 52% of recruiters said that they had encountered candidates who lied about their skills on their CVs, and 38 % of recruiters said that an applicant had been coached in advance on standard interview questions (HireVue, 2023). Without a goal, tamper-resistant skills checking measure early into the recruitment pipeline, organizations run the risk of propelling the wrongly qualified or dishonest applicant to an expensive and time-sensitive interview. These are the challenges that HireIQ is dealing with. The platform replaces the subjective manual screening process with a reproducible, data-driven pipeline, enforced server-side with anti-cheating measures, and with an anti-cheating hybrid NLP-based CV ranking engine (TF-IDF + BERT semantic similarity) which can be applied to any job role and industry. The result is a more expedited, unbiased and precise first round evaluation procedure that costs less, lowers recruiter load, and reveals the most truly qualified applicants. 

3.Project Scope 

3.1 Proposed Work 

HireIQ is a full-stack web site that integrates the totality of the recruitment pre-screening 
experience, inclusive of public job browsing, authenticated job posting, AI-based quiz-based 
assessment, secure, curricula vitae (CV) uploading of qualified applicants, a hybrid natural 
language processing (NLP)-based CV ranking system, and a structured recruiter dashboard. The 
system is designed to accommodate any job position and sector with AI generated content being 
dynamically tailored to fit the description of the specific job.

3.2 In-Scope Features 

1.  Public job listings homepage with search and filter by title, location, and job type, no login required to browse view. 
2. Authentication is performed using Google OAuth 2.0, cross-site request forgery (CSRF) state token verification is used to prevent the loss of the session, and the user is allowed to register without going through steps of manual registration. 
3. Authenticated users can post job vacancies with title, company, description, location, job type, and required skills. 
4. AI quiz generation (Google Gemini 2.5 Flash) of 15 questions per job posting, each applicant receives a randomized selection of 15 with shuffled answers. 
5. The quiz has a time limit of five minutes on the server which is strictly observed; otherwise the submissions beyond the time-limit are rejected and scored zero. 
6. Tab-switch detection during quiz 3 switches trigger automatic submission. 
7. Server-side quiz scoring correct answers are never transmitted to the frontend. 
8. CV upload pipeline gated behind a passing quiz score (12/15 or above), with retry limits and cooldown timers. 
9. Each PDF CV upload is checked through a complete six-tier security validation pipeline, which makes sure that all the data uploads meet the data integrity and privacy criteria.  
10. TF-IDF keyword matches scoring (32% weight) and BERT semantic similarity scoring (48% weight) per CV against the job description. (Combined hybrid ranking formula: Quiz 20% + TF-IDF 32% + BERT 48%.) 
11. TF-IDF keyword matches scoring (32% weight) and BERT semantic similarity scoring (48% weight) per CV against the job description. (Combined hybrid ranking formula: Quiz 20% + TF-IDF 32% + BERT 48%.) 
12. Gemini 2.5 Flash generates a plain English explanatory narrative of every applicant, falling back to rule-based content availability. 
13. Real-time WebSocket ranking progress for live scoring updates as CVs are processed. 
14. Resume viewer with dual-layer keyword and semantic sentence highlighting. 
15. CSV export of full applicant rankings including quiz scores. 


3.3 Out-of-Scope Features 


1. The system will not integrate with physical hardware devices such as webcams, biometric scanners or any external IoT/HR hardware. 
2. The system will not comply with enterprise-level regulations such as full GDPR/CCPA audits or ISO 27001 certification processes. 
3. The system will not include monetization or commercial features such as job ad marketplaces or advertising integrations. 
4. The system will not implement deep behavioral fraud detection such as keystroke dynamics, mouse movement analytics or webcam proctoring. 
5. The system will not support multiple languages (English only in this version.) 
6. The system will not connect to external corporate systems such as HRIS, LDAP/Active Directory or payroll/ERP platforms. 

7.Possible Future Improvements 


1.Development of a native mobile application for Android and iOS to allow candidates 
to browse jobs, take quizzes and track application status on mobile devices, with push 
notifications for job posters when new CVs are submitted. 

2.Expansion to support skills-based hiring through dynamic skill graphing, automated

personalized job recommendations based on a candidate's existing skill profile.

3.Development of a predictive hire success scoring model trained on historical hiring 
outcomes to forecast a candidate's long-term performance in a role beyond the initial 
CV and quiz assessment. 

4.Addition of a practical skills assessment module supporting role-specific coding 
challenges, scenario-based tasks and short-answer questions with AI evaluation, 
allowing job posters to verify hands-on technical ability before progressing candidates 
to final interviews.   
 




1. Functional Requirements 
 
1.1 User Functional Requirements 

1 Users must be able to browse and view all job postings on the 
homepage without logging in. 

2 Users must be able to search and filter jobs by title, location, and job 
type on the homepage. 

3 When visitors click Apply or Post a Job, the system must redirect 
them to the login page then return them to their intended action after login. 

4 
Users must be able to login via Google OAuth 2.0 with CSRF state 
token validation; no manual registration required. 

5 Any logged-in user must be able to post a job with title, company, 
description, location, job type, and required skills. 

6 Any logged-in user must be able to apply for any job posted by 
another user; a user must not be able to apply for their own posting. 

7 When a logged-in user clicks Apply, the system must present a 10-
question MCQ quiz that the applicant must complete before submitting a CV. 

8 Applicants must be able to navigate between quiz questions and 
submit their answers within the allotted time. 

9 Applicants scoring 12 or above out of 15 must be granted access to 
the CV upload page for that specific job. 

10 Applicants scoring below 7 must be shown a 24-hour retry 
countdown with a maximum of 2 attempts per job per user. 

11 Applicants must be able to upload their CV in PDF format after 
passing the quiz for that job. 

12 Applicants must be able to view their own application status per job: 
Quiz Passed, CV Uploaded, or Under Review. 
 
13 Job posters must be able to view all applicants for their own posting 
ranked by the combined hybrid score, including quiz score, tab 
switch count, AI scores, skill gaps, and AI explanation. 

14 Job posters must be able to view individual CV content with dual-
layer highlighting: green for matched skill keywords and yellow for BERT-matched sentences. 

15 Job posters must be able to export all applicant rankings including 
quiz scores as a downloadable CSV file. 
 

User Functional Requirements 

 
1.2 System Functional Requirements 

1 Job listings must display title, company, location, job type, and a short 
description preview on the public homepage. 
 
2 The system must automatically generate a 10-question MCQ quiz using 
Google Gemini 2.5 Flash based on the job description and industry 
when an applicant clicks Apply. 

3 Gemini must generate 20 questions per job; each applicant must receive 
a randomized selection of 15 with shuffled answer options so no two 
applicants see the same quiz. 
 
4 The quiz must enforce a server-side 10-minute timer; submissions 
received after 300 seconds must be rejected and scored zero regardless 
of frontend state. 

5 The system must detect and record tab switches during the quiz; 3 
switches must trigger automatic quiz submission with the current answers. 

6 All quiz answers must be scored entirely on the server side; the correct 
answers must never be transmitted to the frontend at any point. 
 
7 Every uploaded PDF must pass through a mandatory 6-layer security 
pipeline before being written to disk. 
 
8 The system must automatically extract full text from uploaded PDFs 
using PyMuPDF at upload time and store it in MongoDB. 

9 The system must rank uploaded CVs based on quiz performance and 
semantic similarity between the CV and job description. 

10 The system must identify matched and missing skills from each CV 
relative to the job description. 

11 The system must generate a plain-language explanation summarizing 
each applicant's strengths and skill gaps. 

System Functional Requirements 
 
2.Non-Functional Requirements 


1 AI models must be loaded once at system startup to prevent repeated initialization and improve performance. 

2 BERT inference per CV must be completed within 10 seconds on CPU hardware without requiring a GPU. 

3 The public job listings homepage must be loaded within 3 seconds for up to 500 active job postings. 
 
4 The quiz timer must be enforced server-side, the frontend countdown is visual only and cannot be manipulated to extend the allowed time. 

5 PDF uploads must be rejected if file size exceeds 5MB, if extracted text is fewer than 50 characters, or if extracted text exceeds 50,000 characters. 

6 All protected API routes must require a valid JWT token, and the unauthenticated requests must receive HTTP 401 and authenticated users accessing others' resources must receive HTTP 403. 

7 API endpoints must implement rate limiting to prevent abuse and denial-of-service attempts. 
 
8 All production traffic must be served over HTTPS TLS 1.2 or higher — HTTP requests must automatically redirect to HTTPS. 

9 Quiz submission must be rate-limited to 5 requests per minute per IP, login to 3 per minute, and job posting to 10 per hour per account using Flask-Limiter. 
 
10 The quiz interface must clearly display the remaining time, current question number, and tab switch warnings in real time throughout the attempt. 

11 All MongoDB operations must be wrapped in try/except with safe return values the raw exceptions and stack traces must never be exposed to the frontend. 
 
12 The system must achieve at least 99% uptime during demonstration and testing phases. 

13 All credentials and API keys must be securely stored outside the source code and must not be hardcoded.
